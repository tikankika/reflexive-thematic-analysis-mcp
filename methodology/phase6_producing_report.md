# Phase 6: Producing the Report in Dialogic RTA

## Purpose

Phase 6 is not a discrete stage that begins when analysis is complete. Writing is analysis. As Byrne emphasizes (2022, p. 1409):

> "The separation between phases five and six can often be blurry. Further, this 'final' phase would rarely only occur at the end of the analysis."

The researcher has been writing throughout — reflexive memos, code definitions, theme development notes. Phase 6 is about producing the formal analytical narrative that tells the story of the data. It is the final inspection and integration of analytical work that has evolved recursively throughout the entire process.

The task, as Braun and Clarke (2006, p. 93) describe it: "to tell the complicated story of your data in a way which convinces the reader of the merit and validity of your analysis."

In dialogic contexts, Phase 6 introduces the most visible risk to analytical quality: the flattening of interpretive depth through AI-assisted writing. AI produces fluent, organized, grammatically polished prose. It also produces prose that is characteristically generic — it sounds like analysis without doing the work of analysis. The researcher must maintain their own analytical voice throughout the write-up, and that voice must carry the interpretive depth that distinguishes reflexive TA from surface-level description.

---

## Analytical Write-Up

Phase 5 established the distinction between illustrative and analytical treatment. Phase 6 is where that distinction becomes consequential. The standard for reflexive TA is analytical: data extracts are integral to the argument, literature is integrated as it becomes relevant, and the narrative interprets rather than describes.

**Byrne's example (Box 4, p. 1408–1409)** demonstrates what this looks like in practice: rather than describing what participants said about the "appropriate educator," he examines *how* participants constructed the criteria (through negative connotations), identifies the pattern of defining appropriateness by what it is *not*, and connects to theoretical concepts (value dissimilarity, negative perceptions of the "other" group). Every sentence does interpretive work.

---

## The AI Writing Risk: Analytical Flattening

AI can assist with drafting. But AI-assisted writing in qualitative research carries a specific risk that is different from the "proving the obvious" problem in coding or theme generation.

**The risk is not that AI writes incorrect things. It is that AI writes analytically empty things that sound correct.**

AI-generated analytical prose has a characteristic signature:
- Fluent transitions that connect ideas without genuine analytical movement
- Accurate description of data presented as if it were interpretation
- Literature references that are decorative rather than generative — cited to show awareness rather than to build argument
- A tone of authoritative synthesis that masks the absence of interpretive depth

The result reads well. It is organized, grammatical, referenced. But it does not do the work of analysis. It describes themes rather than arguing for interpretations. It presents data rather than interrogating what the data means.

**This matters because the write-up is the analysis.** In qualitative research, the written narrative is not a report of findings that exist independently — it is the medium through which findings are constructed. If the writing lacks interpretive depth, the analysis lacks interpretive depth. There is no analysis "behind" the text that the text fails to convey. The text *is* the analysis.

### Countermeasures

**Write the analytical argument first, without AI.** Articulate what each theme means, why it matters, and how it connects to theory in your own words, before using AI for anything. This establishes the analytical core in the researcher's voice.

**Use AI for mechanical tasks, not interpretive ones.** AI can help with formatting, citation checking, language polishing, structural organization. It should not generate the interpretive substance.

**Test every paragraph.** Does this paragraph make an analytical argument — or does it describe a finding? Does it interpret — or report? Does it say something that requires this specific data to be true — or could it be said about any study on this topic?

**Read your own prose aloud.** The researcher's analytical voice has texture — hesitation where things are uncertain, emphasis where things are surprising, specificity where things are grounded in particular data. AI prose is uniformly smooth. If your write-up reads uniformly smooth, AI may have displaced your voice.

## Ordering Themes

Theme ordering was planned in Phase 5. In the writing process, the planned order may need revision — an order that seemed logical in the abstract may not produce the most compelling narrative when the actual prose is written. The test is whether each theme builds on what came before and sets up what follows, creating a coherent arc rather than a sequence of independent sections.

---

## Balancing Extracts and Analysis

**Sufficient evidence:** Enough extracts to demonstrate each theme convincingly. Multiple perspectives — different participants, different focus groups — showing both the coherence and diversity of the pattern.

**But analysis must dominate.** If you removed all extracts, you should still have a coherent, substantial analytical argument. The extracts support the argument — the argument does not depend on the extracts for its existence.

**Introducing extracts:** Strong framing tells the reader what to see in the extract and why it matters. Weak framing drops the quote without context. The difference:

*Strong:* "One teacher's account powerfully captured the tension between aspiration and constraint, revealing how time pressure functions not as simple absence but as an active force reshaping professional priorities: [extract]. This construction of time as agent — something that *does things* to practice rather than something simply lacking — recurred across all three focus groups."

*Weak:* "A participant said: [extract]."

## Transparency About AI Use

The Methods section must articulate how AI was used — not as disclosure of a limitation, but as description of a methodology. Dialogic RTA is a specific analytical approach with its own logic, safeguards, and quality criteria. Describing it clearly is a contribution to the emerging methodological literature on AI in qualitative research.

The framing matters. "AI did it and I checked" describes a quality assurance process. What actually happened — a sustained dialogue where AI's computational proposals were evaluated, challenged, and often rejected through the researcher's interpretive engagement — is a *method*. The write-up should make this method visible: which phases involved AI, what AI's role was, how the researcher maintained interpretive authority, and what the reflexive documentation reveals about AI's influence on the analysis. The reader should understand not just that AI was used but how it was used to produce more transparent, more explicitly reasoned analysis than traditional approaches often achieve.

---

## Methodological Integrity in the Write-Up

The write-up demonstrates methodological integrity when:

- **Epistemological consistency:** Language and claims align with constructionist approach. Themes are presented as constructions, not discoveries. The researcher is positioned as active interpreter, not passive reporter.
- **Interpretive depth:** The analysis goes substantially beyond description. Each theme makes arguments, not just statements.
- **Researcher positioning:** The researcher's interpretive authority is visible throughout. The reader can see that a human being — with specific knowledge, commitments, and analytical sensibility — produced this analysis.
- **AI transparency:** AI's role is visible without being defensive. The researcher demonstrates how AI augmented rather than replaced interpretive work.
- **Reflexive awareness:** Both interpretive reflexivity and AI-mediation reflexivity are evident — not as separate sections, but woven into the analytical fabric.

## For Focus Group Data

Focus group analysis involves specific considerations in the write-up:

**Show patterns across groups.** Where a pattern appears across multiple focus groups, this is analytically significant — the pattern transcends the dynamics of any single group.

**Acknowledge group dynamics where relevant.** How participants built on each other's contributions, what was safe to say in the group, where consensus formed or dissipated.

**Compare and contrast.** Different groups may express the same theme differently, or certain themes may be prominent in some groups but absent in others. These differences are analytically interesting.

## For Multilingual Data

**Preserve the original language.** Provide both the original (e.g., Swedish) and translation in the write-up. The original preserves culturally specific meaning that translation may flatten.

**Note translation choices.** Where terms lack direct equivalents, explain the nuance. Translation is interpretive work — making it visible strengthens rather than weakens the analysis.

---

## Recursive Writing

Writing is not a single pass. As Byrne notes (2022, p. 1409): "As codes and themes change and evolve over the course of the analysis, so too can the write-up."

Writing often generates new analytical insights. The researcher may discover, while trying to articulate a theme, that the definition needs revision — or that two themes are actually one — or that a sub-theme deserves independent status. This is not failure. It is evidence that writing is functioning as analysis.

Track major revisions. The evolution of the write-up is part of the audit trail.

---

## Completion

The analysis is complete when:

- The written narrative tells a coherent, compelling, non-repetitive story of the data
- Each theme makes an analytical argument grounded in data and connected to theory
- The balance between extracts and analysis is appropriate
- Research questions are clearly addressed
- The researcher's interpretive voice is distinct and sustained throughout
- AI's role is transparent and the analysis demonstrates genuine human interpretive engagement
- The researcher can defend every interpretive choice
