## PART 1: Self-Reflection Questions for the Researcher

### Understanding the Continuum

**The Key Distinction (from Byrne 2022 & Braun & Clarke 2006):**

**SEMANTIC CODING:**
- Identifies EXPLICIT or SURFACE meanings
- Descriptive analysis
- Does NOT examine beyond what participant said/wrote
- Presents content AS COMMUNICATED by participant
- Progression from description → interpretation of significance
- Question: "What did they say?"

**LATENT CODING:**
- Goes BEYOND descriptive level
- Identifies HIDDEN meanings, underlying assumptions, ideas, ideologies
- Examines what SHAPES or INFORMS the semantic content
- More interpretive and creative
- Requires active researcher role
- Question: "What assumptions underlie what they said?"

**The Jelly Analogy (from Braun & Clarke 2006):**
> "If we imagine our data three-dimensionally as an uneven blob of jelly, the semantic approach would seek to describe the surface of the jelly, its form and meaning, while a latent approach would seek to identify the features that gave it that particular form and meaning."

### Question 3: Can you "see" your codes in the data?

This is the crucial test:

**SEMANTIC codes:**
- Someone else reading the transcript could point to WHERE you got this code
- The code is "visible" in the participant's language
- Example: Teacher says "I worry about privacy" → Code: "Privacy concerns about AI"
- Another researcher might use different words, but would see the same content

**LATENT codes:**
- Another researcher might NOT identify the same code
- The code represents YOUR interpretation of underlying meaning
- Example: Teacher says "I worry about privacy" → Code: "Surveillance capitalism anxiety"
- This interprets what MIGHT underlie the concern, but teacher didn't say this
