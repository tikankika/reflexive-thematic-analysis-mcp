# Inductive versus Deductive Analysis in Dialogic RTA

---

## The Starting Point

Braun and Clarke are explicit: coding and analysis rarely fall cleanly into one approach. Purely inductive analysis is not possible — the researcher always brings frameworks, even if only the research questions themselves. Purely deductive analysis is not possible in reflexive TA — the researcher must remain responsive to the data. The question is which approach predominates, and what that means for the analytical work.

---

## The Continuum

**Inductive (data-driven, bottom-up):** Codes emerge from the researcher's engagement with the data rather than from pre-existing theoretical frameworks. The research questions define the domain — what to attend to — but do not prescribe what will be found. Coding is open: the researcher asks *what is happening here?* rather than *where do I see Theory X?* The result is rich description of the breadth of the dataset.

**Deductive (theory-driven, top-down):** Codes are developed relative to a conceptual or theoretical framework. The researcher brings structured expectations about what patterns to look for. Analysis focuses on specific aspects of the data interpreted through a particular theoretical lens. The result is detailed theoretical analysis of targeted phenomena, potentially at the cost of breadth.

**The hybrid reality:** Most analyses use a combination. The common formulation is "predominantly inductive, theoretically sensitised" — meaning codes grow from data, but the researcher's familiarity with relevant literature shapes what they notice. Theoretical sensitisation is not the same as deductive coding: it means the researcher's reading has attuned their attention, not that they are applying a predetermined coding frame.

---

## What Each Approach Does

**Inductive analysis** prioritises respondent-based meanings over researcher-based meanings. Codes use participants' language and concepts. Themes are organised around patterns that the data itself makes visible. Literature enters primarily in the Discussion, where inductively generated findings are connected to existing knowledge. The strength is openness to the unexpected — patterns the researcher did not anticipate and that existing theory does not predict.

**Deductive analysis** prioritises researcher-based meanings informed by theory. Codes operationalise theoretical constructs. Themes are organised around the theoretical framework. Literature is integrated throughout the Results, as findings are interpreted in theoretical terms from the outset. The strength is theoretical contribution — direct engagement with existing debates and frameworks.

**The trade-off** is between richness and depth. Inductive analysis captures the full landscape but may lack theoretical precision. Deductive analysis achieves theoretical depth but may miss important patterns outside its frame. Braun and Clarke (2020) describe this as "rich description of the overall dataset" versus "detailed analysis of a particular aspect."

---

## Research Questions as Diagnostic

The formulation of research questions often reveals the actual analytical approach, regardless of what the researcher claims.

**Inductive research questions** define a domain to explore: *"How do teachers experience AI integration?"* This tells the researcher what to attend to (teachers, AI, experience) but not what they will find. The question is genuinely open.

**Deductive research questions** embed theoretical constructs: *"How do perceived benefits and barriers affect AI adoption?"* This already assumes a benefits/barriers framework. Codes will be organised around this structure. The question is theoretically pre-structured.

**Theoretically sensitised questions** fall between: *"How do teachers construct meaning around AI's impact on knowledge and learning?"* This defines both domain (AI, knowledge, learning) and analytical lens (meaning construction) without predetermining findings. The theoretical sensitisation (constructionism, sensemaking) shapes attention without dictating codes.

The researcher should examine their own research questions honestly: do they define a territory to explore, or do they embed a theoretical structure to apply?

## The AI Dimension

AI's relationship to the inductive-deductive continuum introduces specific analytical risks.

**AI has no genuinely inductive capacity.** When AI proposes codes, it draws on patterns from training data — patterns derived from how similar data has been analysed in published research. AI's "inductive" proposals are actually deductive in a specific sense: they apply frameworks learned from existing literature to new data. When AI proposes a benefits/barriers structure for data about technology in education, it is not discovering this structure in the data — it is applying the most common analytical framework from its training corpus.

**This matters most for inductive analysis.** If the researcher intends predominantly inductive work, AI's proposals represent the strongest gravitational pull toward deductive structure. Every AI-proposed code carries implicit theoretical assumptions from published research. The researcher conducting inductive analysis must be particularly vigilant about evaluating whether AI's proposals capture what *this* data says or what AI's training data expects data like this to say.

**For deductive analysis, AI can be more straightforwardly useful.** If the researcher is explicitly applying a theoretical framework, AI can help identify where data reflects theoretical constructs. The risk is different: not that AI imposes unwanted theory, but that AI confirms theoretical expectations too readily, missing data that challenges or complicates the framework. The researcher needs AI to surface disconfirming evidence as actively as confirming evidence.

**The "theoretically sensitised" position requires the most careful AI management.** The researcher wants theory to inform attention without determining codes. AI's proposals will tend to push toward one end or the other — either fully theorised codes (deductive) or surface-level descriptive codes (pseudo-inductive). The researcher must calibrate: accepting theoretical resonance when it genuinely illuminates the data, rejecting it when it flattens situated meaning into familiar categories.

## Coherence with Epistemological Commitments

The inductive-deductive choice must cohere with the positions established in epistemology and orientation.

**Constructionist + Experiential + Inductive** is the classic reflexive TA alignment. Meaning is socially constructed, the researcher prioritises participant constructions, and codes grow from how participants actually construct their accounts. This combination produces rich experiential description grounded in participant meaning-making. It is the most internally consistent configuration for exploratory research into understudied phenomena.

**Constructionist + Critical + Deductive** is the classic critical discourse alignment. Meaning is socially constructed, the researcher interrogates discursive mechanisms, and theoretical frameworks guide the identification of ideological patterns. This combination produces theoretically sophisticated analysis of how discourse functions.

**Constructionist + Experiential + Theoretically Sensitised** occupies productive middle ground. The researcher's theoretical reading shapes attention — noticing when participants construct meaning in ways that resonate with sensemaking theory, or when accounts reveal epistemological assumptions about knowledge and learning — without imposing these theories as coding categories. Codes grow from data; theory helps the researcher recognise what is analytically interesting about what grows.

Incoherent combinations exist. Claiming predominantly inductive analysis while organising all codes around a predetermined theoretical framework is a methodological contradiction. Claiming deductive analysis while having no explicit theoretical framework is equally incoherent. The researcher should verify that their actual coding practice matches their claimed approach.

---

## Implications Across Phases

**Phase 2a (Initial Coding):** In inductive work, coding is open — the researcher reads closely and codes what they see, using participant language. In deductive work, the theoretical framework provides a coding structure. In theoretically sensitised work, coding is open but the researcher's theoretical awareness shapes what they notice. AI's proposals are evaluated against the chosen approach: inductive coding that consistently uses theoretical terminology has drifted deductive.

**Phase 3 (Generating Themes):** Inductive theme generation organises codes around patterns that emerge from the code set. Deductive theme generation organises codes around theoretical constructs. The most common AI risk here is premature theoretical structuring — AI proposing thematic frameworks that mirror published literature rather than reflecting the patterns in the researcher's actual codes.

**Phase 5–6 (Defining, Naming, Writing):** In inductive analysis, literature typically enters the Discussion, where empirical findings are connected to existing knowledge. In deductive analysis, literature is integrated throughout Results, as findings are interpreted theoretically from the outset. Theme names reflect this: inductive names tend toward data-grounded description, deductive names toward theoretical concepts. AI-generated theme names almost always lean deductive — they sound like literature review categories rather than data-driven constructions.
