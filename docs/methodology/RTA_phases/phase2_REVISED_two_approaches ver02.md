# Phase 2: Generating Initial Codes - TWO VIABLE APPROACHES

## Critical Methodological Choice

The literature and emerging practice reveal TWO legitimate approaches to AI-augmented coding:

**APPROACH A: Human-First Coding** (Traditional - Byrne 2022, Braun & Clarke 2006)
- Human generates initial codes
- AI scaffolds refinement

**APPROACH B: AI-First Coding** (Emerging - Naeem et al. 2025, NguyenTrung 2025)
- AI generates initial codes  
- Human critically evaluates and refines

**BOTH are methodologically sound IF reflexivity and interpretive authority are maintained.**

## APPROACH B: AI-First Coding (YOUR APPROACH!)

### When to Use:
- Working with large datasets
- Want systematic initial coverage
- Prefer evaluating/refining over generating from scratch
- Comfortable with critical evaluation mode
- **Want to work with whole transcript at once**

### Theoretical Justification:

**Naeem et al. (2025):**
> "AI to select keywords and quotations from the data... AI to suggest code names based on the instructions... this AI capability... can capture a broader range of expressions and nuances"

**NguyenTrung (2025):**
Shows AI can generate preliminary codes that researchers then evaluate - the human's critical evaluation MAINTAINS reflexivity

**Key Principle:**
AI-first doesn't reduce reflexivity IF:
1. Human maintains critical stance
2. Decisions about accepting/rejecting/modifying AI codes are documented
3. Human's theoretical positioning guides evaluation
4. The evaluation process is transparent

## APPROACH B: Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Prepare AI for Whole-Transcript Coding

**Context-Setting Prompt:**

```markdown
I am conducting Reflexive Thematic Analysis on focus group transcripts about 
[your topic]. I have completed deep familiarization with the data (Phase 1).

I need you to generate INITIAL CODES for an entire transcript. This is 
preliminary coding - I will critically evaluate and refine all codes.

My epistemological position: [Constructionist]
My research questions:
1. [RQ1]
2. [RQ2]

Coding guidelines:
- Generate codes in [Swedish/original language]
- Use underscores for multi-word codes: #code_example
- Mark in vivo codes with quotes: #"exact_participant_phrase"
- Include both semantic (surface meaning) and latent (underlying) codes
- Code systematically through ENTIRE transcript
- Codes should be specific enough to stand alone

Format your output as:
**[SPEAKER]:** [Quote]
#code1 #code2 #"in_vivo_code" (brief explanation if latent)

Ready? I will upload the transcript now.
```

### Step 2: Upload Transcript and Receive AI Initial Coding

**Upload your complete transcript to AI**

AI will return coded version like:
```markdown
**[SPEAKER_03]:** [Full quote about verification]
#authenticity_concerns #oral_verification #"skrivbordsprodukt" 
#understanding_as_knowledge #uncertainty
```

### Step 3: Critical Human Evaluation (THE ESSENTIAL STEP!)

**THIS is where your reflexivity and interpretive authority happen!**

Create an evaluation document:

```markdown
# AI-Generated Codes - Critical Evaluation
## Transcript: [ID]
## Date: [Date]
## Evaluator: [Your name]

---

### Section 1: [First passage]

AI Codes Generated:
- #code1
- #code2  
- #"in_vivo_code"

My Critical Evaluation:

| AI Code | Accept? | Modify? | Reject? | My Decision & Rationale |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|-------------------------|
| #authenticity_concerns | ✓ | - | - | Accurate semantic code |
| #oral_verification | ✓ | - | - | Captures key practice |
| #"skrivbordsprodukt" | ✓ | - | - | Important in vivo term |
| #understanding_as_knowledge | - | ✓ | - | Refine to #förståelse_som_kunskap (maintain Swedish) |
| #uncertainty | - | - | ✓ | Too vague - better captured by #osäker_position |

Codes AI MISSED that I see:
- #binär_bedömning (either/or thinking) - latent pattern I interpret
- Rationale: AI coded semantically but I see underlying dichotomous logic

Reflexive Notes:
- Why did I disagree with #understanding_as_knowledge code?
  Because: [Your reasoning about language/cultural meaning]
- What does my rejection of #uncertainty tell me about my interpretation?
  That: [Your reflexive insight]
```

### Step 4: Generate YOUR Refined Code List

**After critical evaluation, create YOUR authoritative code list:**

```markdown
# My Refined Codes - Transcript [ID]

## Final Codes (After Critical Evaluation):

### Codes from AI that I accepted:
1. #authenticity_concerns - [Brief description]
2. #oral_verification - [Brief description]
3. #"skrivbordsprodukt" - In vivo, means [explanation]

### Codes from AI that I modified:
4. #förståelse_som_kunskap (was #understanding_as_knowledge)
   - Why modified: [Maintain original language, cultural specificity]

### Codes from AI that I rejected:
- #uncertainty - Rejected because: [Too vague]

### Codes I added (AI missed):
5. #binär_bedömning - [My interpretation of latent pattern]
   - Why added: [Your reasoning]

### Codes needing second-pass evaluation:
6. #code_X - Uncertain if relevant, check against RQs in next iteration
```

### Step 5: Second-Pass Evaluation (ESSENTIAL!)

**Read through entire transcript again WITH your refined codes**

Purpose:
- Verify codes work across whole transcript
- Check for consistency
- Identify what AI missed
- Confirm your interpretive decisions

```markdown
# Second-Pass Evaluation

Reading transcript again with my refined codes...

Additional observations:
- Pattern X appears more than AI coded - add #new_code
- Code Y from AI doesn't actually fit all instances - remove
- Two AI codes (#A and #B) are actually same concept - consolidate

Changes made:
| Original | Action | Result | Rationale |
|----------|--------|--------|-----------|
| #code_A + #code_B | Consolidate | #unified_code | [Why] |
| Added | New | #pattern_X | [What I noticed] |
```

## The Critical Dialogic Process

### Your Evaluation IS the Reflexive Analysis

**The power of AI-first approach:**

When you **critically evaluate** AI codes, you must:
1. **Articulate WHY** you accept/reject/modify
2. **Explain your interpretation** that differs from AI
3. **Make your theoretical positioning visible**
4. **Document your meaning-making process**

This actually ENHANCES reflexivity because your interpretive decisions become explicit!

### Reflexive Questions to Ask:

For EACH AI-generated code:

```markdown
1. Does this code align with my research questions?
   - If no: Reject
   - If yes: Continue evaluation

2. Does this code capture meaning as I understand it from familiarization?
   - If no: What's missing? Modify or add new code
   - If yes: Accept

3. Is this semantic or latent coding?
   - Semantic: Does it accurately describe surface meaning?
   - Latent: Does it appropriately interpret underlying meaning?
   - Is balance appropriate?

4. Does this code work in original language context?
   - Cultural meanings preserved?
   - In vivo language honored?

5. What does my agreement/disagreement reveal about my interpretation?
   - Why do I see it differently?
   - What theoretical lens am I applying?
   - What am I prioritizing in the data?
```

## Comparing the Two Approaches

### Both Approaches Require:
- ✅ Systematic attention to entire dataset
- ✅ Multiple iterations
- ✅ Reflexive documentation
- ✅ Clear, self-explanatory codes
- ✅ Human interpretive authority
- ✅ Both semantic and latent coding

### Key Differences:

| Aspect | Human-First | AI-First |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Starting point | Blank slate | AI initial codes |
| Cognitive mode | Generative | Evaluative |
| Reflexivity focus | Discovering patterns | Interrogating patterns |
| Time investment | High (generation) | Moderate (evaluation) |
| Risk | Missing patterns | Passive acceptance |
| Benefit | Deep immersion | Systematic coverage |
| Best for | Small datasets, novices | Large datasets, experienced |

## Quality Indicators (SAME for Both Approaches!)

### Strong AI-First Coding Shows:
- ✅ **Critical evaluation documented** for ALL AI codes
- ✅ **Modifications made** - evidence of human interpretation
- ✅ **Codes added** - what AI missed but human saw
- ✅ **Codes rejected** - with clear rationale
- ✅ **Reflexive logs complete** - awareness of decisions
- ✅ **Second-pass verification** - consistency check
- ✅ **Interpretive authority clear** - these are YOUR codes

### Weak AI-First Coding Shows:
- ❌ Accepting AI codes without evaluation
- ❌ No modifications or additions
- ❌ No documentation of decision-making
- ❌ Single pass only
- ❌ Unable to explain why codes fit
- ❌ Passive relationship to AI output

## Working with Whole Transcript (Your Insight!)

### Advantages of AI-First for Whole-Transcript Work:

**1. Gestalt Understanding:**
```markdown
AI codes entire transcript → You see WHOLE pattern
vs.
Coding line-by-line → Might lose overall flow
```

**2. Comparative Evaluation:**
```markdown
"How does this speaker's section compare to others?"
- Easier to see with AI codes across all speakers
- Then refine based on your interpretation
```

**3. Systematic Coverage:**
```markdown
AI ensures every part attended to
→ You verify relevance
→ Add nuanced interpretation
```

**4. Efficiency with Depth:**
```markdown
AI provides breadth (systematic coverage)
+ Human provides depth (interpretive refinement)
= Comprehensive AND reflexive coding
```

## Literature Support

**Naeem et al. (2025) on AI-First:**
> "Step 3 Coding: During this stage it is necessary to instruct AI to suggest 
> code names based on the instructions"

Shows AI can generate initial codes, then human evaluates and refines through "Guided AI Thematic Analysis"

**Ozuem et al. (2025) on Documentation:**
> "Researchers working in AI-augmented contexts are therefore advised to 
> document both human-led and AI-supported analytic actions in detail"

**Critical Point - Prescott et al. (2024):**
Found human-generated codes MORE reliable than AI - **BUT** this doesn't invalidate AI-first IF human critically evaluates and refines!
