# Phase 4: Reviewing Potential Themes in AI-Augmented RTA
## Based on Byrne (2022)

---

## The Critical Work of Review and Refinement

Phase 4 is where the significant analytical work of theme refinement happens. As Byrne notes (2022, p. 1404): "At this phase, it is not uncommon to find that some candidate themes may not function well as meaningful interpretations of the data, or may not provide information that addresses the research question(s)."

This is NOT a mechanical checking process. Phase 4 requires you to critically interrogate your candidate themes and, often, fundamentally reconceptualize how you're organizing meaning in your data.

---

## Braun & Clarke's Key Questions for Theme Review

**1. Is this a theme (or is it just a code)?**

**2. If it is a theme, what is the quality of this theme?**

**3. What are the boundaries of this theme?**

**4. Are there enough meaningful data to support this theme?**

**5. Are the data too diverse and wide ranging?**

---

## Two Levels of Review

### Level One: Reviewing Coded Data in Relation to Each Theme

At this level, you review all the coded data extracts that inform each candidate theme.

**The process**:
1. For each candidate theme, gather all the codes and data extracts
2. Re-read all these data extracts together
3. Ask: Do these data items genuinely cohere around the central organizing concept?

**Possible outcomes**: The theme works well, needs adjustment, or doesn't work and needs reconceptualization.

### Level Two: Reviewing Themes in Relation to the Entire Dataset

**Key questions for Level Two**:
- Do the themes together provide a convincing interpretation of the dataset?
- Do they address your research questions adequately?
- Are there gaps—important patterns that no theme captures?
- Are themes clearly distinguishable from each other?

---

## Observing Patton's Dual Criteria

### Internal Homogeneity
Within each theme, data items and codes should cohere meaningfully.

### External Heterogeneity
Between themes, there should be clear distinctions. Each theme should capture something distinctive.

---

## Byrne's Example: Substantial Restructuring

Byrne's example shows how substantial revisions can be:

**Original candidate theme**: "Factors Inhibiting Wellbeing Promotion"

**Problems identified**:
- Very dense and unwieldy
- Boundaries difficult to identify
- Lacked coherence
- Too descriptive

**Solution**: Complete reconceptualization into three separate themes:
1. "The influence of time"
2. "Incompletely theorised agreements"
3. "The axiology of wellbeing promotion"

## Quality Indicators: Phase 4 Complete

**About individual themes**:
✓ Each theme tells a coherent story
✓ Central organizing concept is clear
✓ Codes within each theme genuinely cohere (internal homogeneity)
✓ Sufficient meaningful data supports each theme

**About the thematic framework**:
✓ Themes are clearly distinctive from each other (external heterogeneity)
✓ Framework addresses your research questions
✓ No major gaps or redundancies
