# Phase 5: Defining and Naming Themes in AI-Augmented RTA
## Based on Byrne (2022)

---

## Moving from Structure to Substance

Phase 5 is where you move from having a solid thematic framework to presenting a detailed analysis of what each theme means. You need to:

- Define precisely what each theme captures
- Craft names that communicate effectively
- Select specific data extracts for your write-up

---

## The Work of Defining Themes

For each theme, answer:

**What is the essence of this theme?** What's the central organizing concept?

**What story does this theme tell?** Themes tell stories about patterns.

**How does this theme relate to your research questions?**

**What are the boundaries?** What does it include/exclude?

**How does it relate to other themes?**

---

## Selecting Data Extracts

**Principles**:
- Extracts should be vivid and compelling
- Use multiple extracts across the dataset
- Show the range of the theme
- Balance evidence with analysis

---

## Illustrative vs. Analytical Write-Up

**Illustrative**: Presents extracts as illustrations. Extract can be removed and write-up still makes sense.

**Analytical**: Goes beyond illustration to deeply analyze meaning. Extract cannot be removed without making write-up incomprehensible.

For reflexive TA, analytical approach is generally more appropriate.

---

## The Art of Naming Themes

**Principles**:
- Concise but informative
- Not too generic ("Challenges")
- Not too specific (that's a code)
- Capture the essence
- Consider creativity

**Byrne's examples**:
- "The Influence of Time" (conceptual, suggests complexity)
- "Incompletely Theorised Agreements" (creative, intriguing)
- "The Axiology of Wellbeing Promotion" (precise theoretical term)

---

## Theme Definition Template

```markdown
## Theme: [Name]

**Central organizing concept**: 
[1-2 sentences]

**What this theme captures**:
[Detailed description]

**Key aspects**:
- [Aspect 1]
- [Aspect 2]

**Relationship to research questions**:
[How it contributes]

**Representative data extracts**:
[Key quotes]
```

---

## Preparing the Narrative Structure

Consider theme ordering:
- Which theme establishes context?
- Which themes build on each other?
- What creates compelling narrative arc?
- Which provides satisfying conclusion?

---

## Quality Check

✓ Each theme has clear central organizing concept
✓ Names are concise, informative, distinctive
✓ Boundaries are clear
✓ Data extracts selected for each theme
✓ Ready for analytical write-up
