# Phase 6: Producing the Report in AI-Augmented RTA
## Based on Byrne (2022)

---

## Writing as Analysis

Phase 6 is not a discrete stage. Writing IS analysis. As Byrne notes: "the write-up of qualitative research is very much interwoven into the entire process of the analysis."

---

## The Task: Telling the Complicated Story

Your write-up must provide:
- A concise, coherent, logical, non-repetitive and interesting account
- Sufficient evidence of themes within the data
- Vivid, compelling extracts
- Analytical narrative that goes beyond description
- Arguments in relation to your research questions

---

## Analytical vs. Illustrative Write-Up

**Illustrative**: Surface-level description. Literature held until Discussion. Extract can be removed and write-up still makes sense.

**Analytical**: Interrogates interpretations. Literature integrated in Results. Extract cannot be removed.

**The test** (Terry et al., 2017):
- Extract removed, write-up makes sense → Illustrative
- Extract removed, write-up incomprehensible → Analytical

---

## Ordering Themes: Building Narrative

Themes should connect logically, building a cogent narrative.

**Byrne's rationale** (example):
1. "Best Practice" first — establishes positivity, eases reader in
2. "Axiology" second — introduces the "yes, but..." reality
3. "Influence of Time" third — establishes constraints
4. "Incompletely Theorised Agreements" fourth — shows consequence
5. "Recognising Educator Wellbeing" last — closing voice

---

## Strong Analytical Narratives

**Don't just describe**: "The participant said X"

**Analyze and interpret**: "The participant's construction of X as [interpretation] suggests [theoretical insight]"

**Elements**:
- Contextualize extracts
- Interpret, don't just report
- Connect to theory
- Build arguments
- Show relationships between themes

---

## Balancing Extracts and Analysis

- Sufficient evidence across dataset
- Multiple perspectives
- But narrative should outweigh quoted material
- If you removed extracts, you should still have coherent argument

---

## AI-Augmented Transparency

**Essential disclosure in Methods**:
- Which AI tools used at which phases
- What tasks AI performed vs. human work
- How you critically evaluated AI contributions
- Process for maintaining interpretive authority

---

## Quality Indicators

✓ Goes substantially beyond description
✓ Coherent story flows logically
✓ Sufficient, diverse, vivid extracts
✓ Good balance between analysis and evidence
✓ Literature meaningfully integrated
✓ Research questions clearly addressed
✓ AI use transparently explained
✓ Your interpretive authority visible

---

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Descriptive rather than analytical** — Always ask "So what?"
2. **Extract overload** — Be selective, make analysis prominent
3. **Disconnected themes** — Show relationships
4. **Ignoring complexity** — Show range and contradictions
5. **Weak RQ connection** — Explicit connections throughout
6. **Unclear AI role** — Clear transparency statement

---

## The Journey Complete

**Phase 1**: Immersion in data
**Phase 2**: Systematic coding
**Phase 3**: Provisional themes
**Phase 4**: Critical review and revision
**Phase 5**: Precise definitions and naming
**Phase 6**: Analytical narratives

Throughout: Reflexive awareness, documented decisions, constructionist epistemology, human interpretation central.
